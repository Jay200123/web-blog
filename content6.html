Mobile & Game Platforms

Mobile Platforms

1.) iOS

The iOS is an operating system created by Apple initially intended for the iPhone but later expanded to support other devices such as iPod Touch, iPad, and Apple TV. It has a vast App Store with over 500,000 applications, and it has been downloaded more than 25 billion times. The iOS is known for its intelligent user interface that uses multi-touch gestures and follows the concept of direct manipulation.

2.) Android

Android is a mobile operating system based on Linux, which is developed by the Open Handset Alliance led by Google. It boasts a sizable community of developers who create apps that enhance the devices' capabilities. The Android Market has over 450,000 applications, and downloads have surpassed 10 billion.

3.) Blackberry OS

Blackberry OS is a mobile operating system designed by Research In Motion (RIM) for its smartphones. This OS is recognized for its innate support for corporate e-mail via MIDP, enabling complete wireless activation and synchronization with Microsoft Exchange and Lotus Domino. According to a research, around 45% of mobile developers were utilizing this platform during the time of publication. Moreover, it offers BlackBerry API classes that allow developers to create applications.

4.) Windows Phone

Microsoft released Windows Phone, a mobile operating system in late 2010, as a successor to the Windows Mobile platform. It is intended for the consumer market, with a new user interface called Metro, which integrates the OS with third-party and other Microsoft services, and hardware control.

Gaming Platforms

1.) PlayStation Gaming Platform

This gaming platform began with the original PlayStation, which was created by Ken Kutaragi, a Sony executive. The console was a result of a collaboration between Nintendo and Sony in 1988, where they worked on a CD-ROM for the Super Famicom. Kutaragi's idea was to create a standalone product that uses CDs instead of cartridges, which eventually led to the birth of the PlayStation. It has since become one of the most successful gaming platforms, with the first console selling 100 million units, and the PlayStation 2 surpassing that with over 155 million units sold. The PlayStation, now in its fifth generation, continues to innovate and push boundaries.

2.) Microsoft Xbox Gaming Platform

Although Microsoft was a late entrant to the gaming platform industry, its Xbox console quickly gained popularity. The first Xbox was released in 2001 and sold 24 million units, while the Xbox 360 sold over 84 million units as of 2014. Today, Xbox is in its ninth generation, with the Series X and Series S consoles released in 2020 catering to different market segments. The Series S is a cheaper alternative to the Series X, with variations in terms of speed, storage, and visual quality.

3.) Nintendo Gaming Platform

Nintendo has been a prominent player in the gaming industry for a long time. The NES and Super Nintendo, its first gaming platforms, brought about a major transformation and helped to make video games mainstream, leading to a highly profitable industry. Nintendo's evolution over the years is noteworthy, as it has moved away from being a primary choice for dedicated gamers and has become more popular among casual and beginner gamers.
